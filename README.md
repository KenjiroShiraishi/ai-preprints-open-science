# 🧠 AI, Preprints, and Open Science  
**Learning, Teaching, and Applying Open Science: An Educational Model Using AI and Preprints**

---

## 🔍 Overview

This repository provides educational resources for exploring how Artificial Intelligence (AI), preprints, and open science can be integrated into learning, teaching, and academic practice.

It offers introductory materials, examples, and practical guidance to support ethical, transparent, and participatory science.

- **OSF Project**: https://osf.io/sjknp  
- **DOI**: [10.17605/OSF.IO/SJKNP](https://doi.org/10.17605/OSF.IO/SJKNP)

---

## ❓ Why This Matters

Generative AI and preprints are rapidly becoming part of everyday academic practice.  
For students, early-career researchers, educators, and independent scholars, understanding their ethical and practical implications is no longer optional — it is becoming essential.

Students may soon become more fluent in using AI tools than their supervisors.  
Preprints are also becoming important entry points for sharing early findings, receiving feedback, and increasing research visibility.

These changes are not only technological. They are cultural.  
They are reshaping how knowledge is produced, reviewed, shared, preserved, and taught.

If educators, reviewers, and senior researchers do not engage with these changes, a generational gap in epistemic authority may emerge.

This repository aims to provide a practical starting point for learning and teaching open science in the age of AI and preprints.

## 🧭 Educational Purpose

This project is not intended to provide a fixed curriculum or authoritative guideline.

Rather, it is a practical educational model showing how open science can be taught and practiced using freely accessible tools such as:

- GitHub
- OSF
- DOIs
- Preprints
- Public documentation
- Transparent revision histories
- AI prompt preservation

The goal is to help learners move from passive consumption of information toward active participation in knowledge creation.
